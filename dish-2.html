Indian Samosa

Here's a simple and delicious Matar Paneer recipe for you, step-by-step:

Ingredients

  • All-purpose flour (maida) - 2 cups [For the Dough]
  • Ajwain (carom seeds) - 1/2 tsp (optional) [For the Dough]
  • Salt - 1/2 tsp[For the Dough]
  • Oil or ghee - 1/4 cup [For the Dough]
  • Water - as needed (for kneading) [For the Dough]
  • Potatoes - 4 medium, boiled, peeled, and cubed
  • Green peas - 1/2 cup (fresh or frozen)
  • Ginger - 1 inch, finely chopped or grated
  • Green chilies - 1-2, finely chopped (optional)
  • Cumin seeds - 1/2 tsp
  • Coriander seeds - 1 tsp, crushed
  • Fennel seeds - 1/2 tsp (optional)
  • Turmeric powder - 1/4 tsp
  • Red chili powder - 1/2 tsp (adjust to taste)
  • Garam masala - 1/2 tsp
  • Amchur powder (dry mango powder) - 1/2 tsp
  • Salt - to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves - 2 tbsp, chopped
  • Oil - 1-2 tbsp for cooking filling
  • Oil - for deep frying

Prepare the Dough

  • In a mixing bowl, add the all-purpose flour, ajwain (carom seeds), and salt.
  • Add the oil or ghee to the flour and mix well with your hands until the mixture resembles bread crumbs. This is important for a crispy samosa crust.
  • Gradually add water, a little at a time, and knead into a firm dough. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.

Prepare the Filling

  • Heat 1-2 tbsp of oil in a pan on medium heat.
  • Add cumin seeds, crushed coriander seeds, and fennel seeds. Let them splutter.
  • Add chopped ginger and green chilies, and saut√© for a few seconds.
  • Add green peas and cook for 2-3 minutes (if using fresh peas, cook until tender).
  • Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, and amchur powder. Mix well.
  • Add the boiled and cubed potatoes. Mash them slightly and mix with the spices.
  • Add salt and chopped coriander leaves. Mix everything well and cook for 2-3 minutes.
  • Turn off the heat and let the filling cool.

Shape the Samosas

  • Divide the dough into equal portions and roll each portion into a ball.
  • Take one ball and roll it into an oval shape, around 6-7 inches in diameter.
  • Take one semi-circle, fold it into a cone shape, and seal the edge with a little water.
  • Fill the cone with 1-2 tbsp of the potato filling.
  • Apply water on the edges and press to seal the cone tightly.
  • Repeat the process with the remaining dough and filling.

Fry the Samosas

  • PHeat oil in a deep frying pan on low to medium heat. The oil should not be too hot; otherwise, the samosas will cook too quickly on the outside and remain uncooked inside.
  • Gently slide a few samosas into the hot oil without overcrowding the pan.
  • Fry the samosas on low heat, turning them occasionally, until they are golden and crisp.
  • Remove the samosas with a slotted spoon and drain them on paper towels to remove excess oil.

Serve

  • Serve the samosas hot with mint chutney, tamarind chutney, or ketchup.
  • Enjoy your homemade crispy and spicy Indian Samosas!
